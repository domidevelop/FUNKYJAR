About

Definition Of Cocktail.

Oxford dictionary definition of cocktail says: cocktail is an alcoholic drink consisting of a spirit or spirits mixed with other ingredients, such as fruit juice or cream. Basically, in modern terms, almost any mixed drink can be reffered as cocktail. The definiton is really wide so you can see why there are indfinite options when making cocktails. It can be a simple mixed drink such as gin and tonic or it can be a more complex drink such as Sex On The Beach that has about seven ingredients. There are endless possibilities and that is the beauty. There is no one must recipe and everyone can have there own way of making a certain cocktail depending on tha taste.

Homemade cocktails.

Our goal here is to give you perfect foundation of everything you need to know to make an awesome cocktail party. There is ton of info on cocktails out there but we will stick to what is most important for you to create great tasting cocktails at home and share them with your friends. The key here is to have fun and elevate your typical drinking experience. Do not worry if you made a mess once in a while or the drink came up bad, try again and with time you will find your own best way of making that perfect Mojito while still having fun. So we have preparaed the list of the essential tools you need and a list of the most apropriate cocktails out there for your home party, but let's first take a look at some basics you need to know to get you started.

Stock you home bar.

You don't have to include everything in your home bar, it should have what you can actually enjoy to drink. We have concentrate our selection to rum and gin. They offer endless options and most of the people enjoy them. To add more varaities, we would add a bottle of vodka, tequila and cachasa. We did not include whiskeys and brandys. Stocking you bar with whiskey can get complicated and expensive as each have their own characteristics so we do not consider it a perfect match for home parties. Brandy also is not essential so we choose to skip it. This does not mean they are bad choices, if you like to drink them and would like to explore more classic cocktails, feel free to add them to your home bar.
For the rum, you need at least two kinds, light and dark one. A light rum will be your workhorse for most of our preffered cocktails, from the daiquiri to the mojito. You can spend as little or as much as you like, though rum tends to be one of the more affordable liquors and that is why it tops our list for home parties. Endless options and affortable prices. As a secondary rum, take your pick of a dark one. If you really like to explore tropical and tiki cocktails, a dark rum is essential. We have some of tropical cocktails in our arsenal.
Gin is our second choice. Not everyone is the fan of gin, but you can make a lot of cocktails with it and the best part is that one good bottle of a London dry gin will do it for the most part. It is the most versatile and can work in everything from a dry martini to a gin and tonic.
Having good budget-friendly bottle of vodka will spread your options even more. Vodka has a clean, transparent flavor and it is used for more cocktails than any other distilled spirit. We have two cocktails ideal for parties but vodka can be mixed with any juice or tonic and you have a great drink in no time. We use tequila for margaritas and you will see you can make plenty of different types of margarita. From classic recipe to many different flavours, it is on you to experiment. The most versatile style is a blanco (or silver) tequila. We have added cachasa to our home bar to be able to make that famous Brazilian drink caipirinha. Although not essential, your party will really stand up if you offer your guests caipirinha too.

Methods for mixing.

There are four basic methods for mixing cocktails: shake, stir, build and blend and all of them are incredibly easy and with a little practice you can also learn them quickly and start making great drinks.
You should shake cocktails when the recipe includes fruit juices, cream liqueurs, simple syrup, sour mix, egg, dairy or any other thick or flavorful mixers. Essentially, use the shake whenever you need to ensure that every ingredient is fully integrated into the finished drink's flavor. When you're shaking up a cocktail, pay attention to how frosty the outside of the shaker gets so you know when it's ready to strain.
Stir cocktails that include distilled spirits or very light mixers only. Stirring is a gentler technique for mixing cocktails, though it's often done for at least 30 seconds, which is longer than the typical shake. It is used to delicately combine the drinks with a perfect amount of dilution from the ice. Whether you are shaking or stirring, in most cases you don't want to serve the same ice you mixed your drink with. The ice is broken down and will not last long, so straining is a necessary step for better drinks.
Building a drink means that you simply pour the ingredients into the serving glass and on top of any previous ingredient and that is it. If you're pouring liquor and mixers directly into the glass, you're building a drink. This technique is used for the most basic of mixed drinks. To "Top" a drink simply means finish off the drink by filling the glass. To muddle means to smash drink ingredients to bring out their essence and you would usually use a muddler. Mojiots and caipirinhas use this technique.
Blending cocktails are a great treat and a fun addition to parties. Just put all ingredients into the blender and blend them good. It takes practice to make perfect mix every time but that is all the fun. If the mix is too thin and watery, you will need to add more ice and blend again. If the mix is too chunky, add one of your liquid ingredients and re-blend. Start small because it's easy to upset the balance and make it too watery (which means you need more ice and the cycle continues).

Measure everything.

If you want to constantly make great cocktails you have to measure ingredients. Unless you are a bartender in a busy place and doing a lot of drinks, you have to measure and make use of a jigger, a two-ended cup that measures shots and half-shots. Our recipes are in imperial system but you can easily transform it in metric system.
Remember, 1 ounce equels 30 mililiters. So, get yourself a 30/60ml or 45/60ml jigger and you can use our recipes in either system.
Measuring ensures that you are creating the cocktail as it was intended. An over or under pour of a single ingredient can throw off the delicate balance of a drink and you might not like it. Also, if you are drinking and mixing, your perception of measurements can be thrown off as the night goes on. And we all know how the party goes and you would also like to taste your drinks for sure!

Home Cocktail Party

This is a blueprint and summery of what you need for an awesome cocktail party. You don't have to stock your bar with dozens of bottles to have great time. Our basic setup will be a bottle of light and dark rum, good bottle of a London dry gin and one or all of these three: good budget-friendly vodka, blanco (or silver) tequila or cachasa depending on your preference. This will give us more then enough options. Choose up to six cocktails you will offer on the night. You don't have to offer all of them on every occasion. Maybe you are in a mood for tequila, so you will offer 3-4 versions of margaritas, tequila shots and additional mojitos just in case. Or another night you got yourself a bottle of good light rum, so you can make rum cocktails and there is still a half of bottle of gin from previous party so gin and tonic is always an option. You see, with this mindset and setup, there are plenty of variations and we can alwas mix it up.
Mixing methods and measuring will come from practice and you will learn quickly when to shake or stir, build or blend. Blending will be useful for us as we can make Piña Colada, daiquiries or margaritas in more quantity, store and have them ready when the party starts. This way we don't have to make every drink individually and we can save time.

Mojito

  • 2 ounces light rum
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 6 to 8 fresh mint leaves
  • Splash club soda
  • 1 lime (cut into two halves)
  • Garnish: mint sprig

Place the sugar, mint leaves, and a splash of club soda into a highball glass. Muddle well to dissolve the sugar and to release the flavor of the mint. Squeeze the juice from both halves of a lime into the glass, dropping one-half into the glass. Add the rum. Stir well. Fill the glass with ice cubes. Top with club soda. Garnish with the mint sprig. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Piña Colada

  • 2 ounces​ white rum
  • 1 ounce ​​pineapple juice
  • 1 ounce ​​cream of coconut
  • 1/4 ounce ​​lime juice (fresh)
  • 1 1/2 cups ice
  • Garnish: maraschino cherry, pineapple wedge

Gather the ingredients. For frozen version, blend all the ingredients with ice until nice and smooth. For blender free version, pour the ingredients into a cocktail shaker filled with ice and shake well. Pour / strain into a chilled mason jar, cocktail glass, or collins glass. Garnish with the pineapple wedge and maraschino cherry. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Cuba Libre

  • 1/2 lime (juiced)
  • 2 ounces light rum
  • 4 ounces cola (Coca-Cola)

Gather the ingredients. Squeeze the juice of half a lime into a collins glass or highball glass. Add ice cubes and pour the rum into the glass. Fill with cola and stir well. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Daiquiri

  • 1 1/2 ounces rum (light)
  • 3/4 ounce ​​lime juice (fresh)
  • 1/4 ounce ​simple syrup

Gather the ingredients. In a cocktail shaker with ice cubes, pour the ingredients. Shake well. Strain into a chilled cocktail glass. Serve and ENJOY! :)
The frozen lime daiquiri is the most basic variation of the original cocktail. It simply takes the same three ingredients and tosses them into a blender with ice. It's very simple and you can add whatever fruits you have on hand to give it a quick twist, most popular being strawberry daiquiri.

Blue Hawaiian

  • 1 1/2 ounces light ​rum
  • 3/4 ounce blue curaçao liqueur
  • 3/4 ounce ​cream of coconut
  • 2 ounces ​pineapple juice
  • 1 lime (cut into two halves)
  • Garnish: maraschino cherry, pineapple wedge

Gather the ingredients. In a mixing glass filled with ice cubes, pour the rum, blue curaçao, cream of coconut, and pineapple juice. Stir well (at least 30 seconds). Strain into a collins glass filled with ice cubes. Garnish with the cherry and pineapple wedge. Serve and ENJOY! :)
For the frozen version, simply pour the ingredients into a blender with 1 cup of cracked ice and blend until smooth.

Mai Tai

  • 1 ounce light rum
  • 1 ounce Jamaican rum
  • 1 ounce lime juice (fresh)
  • 1/2 ounce orange curaçao
  • 1/2 ounce orgeat syrup
  • 1/4 ounce simple syrup
  • Garnish: lime shell, ​mint sprig

Gather the ingredients. In a cocktail shaker filled with about 2 cups of crushed ice, pour the ingredients. Shake well. Pour everything (do not strain) into an old-fashioned glass. Garnish with a lime shell sunk into the ice and a sprig of fresh mint. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Hurricane

  • 2 ounces light rum
  • 2 ounces dark rum
  • 2 ounces passion fruit juice
  • 1/2 lime
  • 1 tablespoon simple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon grenadine
  • Garnish: maraschino cherry, orange slice

Gather the ingredients. In a cocktail shaker filled with ice, squeeze the juice from half a lime. Pour the remaining ingredients into the shaker. Strain into a hurricane glass. Shake well, until the outside of the shaker tin becomes frosty. Garnish with a cherry and an orange slice. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Dark 'n' Stormy

  • 2 ounces good dark rum
  • 3 ounces ginger beer
  • Optional: 1/2 ounce lime juice (fresh)
  • Garnish: lime wedge

Gather the ingredients. Build the rum, ginger beer and lime juice in a highball glass filled with ice cubes. Garnish with the lime wedge. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Tom Collins

  • 1 1/2 ounces gin
  • 1 ounce lemon juice
  • 1/2 ounce simple syrup
  • 3 ounces club soda (or to fill)
  • Garnish: maraschino cherry, lemon or orange slice

Gather the ingredients. In a collins glass with ice cubes, pour the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup. Stir thoroughly. Top with club soda. Garnish with a cherry and an orange or lemon slice. You can pin the cherry to the citrus fruit using a cocktail pick and create a garnish known as a "flag" as well. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Classic Gin Martini

  • 2 1/2 ounces gin
  • 1/2 ounce dry vermouth
  • Garnish: 1 or 3 olives or a lemon twist
  • Optional: ​1 dash orange or Angostura bitters

In a mixing glass filled with ice cubes, combine the gin and vermouth, pouring more or less vermouth to your taste. Stir for 30 seconds. Strain into a chilled cocktail glass. Add a dash of orange or Angostura bitters, if desired. Garnish with olives or a lemon twist. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Gin Gin Mule

  • 8 mint leaves
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 3/4 ounce lime juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 ounce simple syrup
  • 1 1/2 ounces gin
  • 1 bottle ginger beer
  • Garnish: sprig of mint

Gather the ingredients. In a cocktail shaker, muddle the mint leaves with lime juice and simple syrup to release the essence of the herbs. Add gin and fill the shaker with ice. Shake well. Strain into a highball glass filled with fresh ice. Top with ginger beer. Garnish with a sprig of mint. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Gin 'n' Tonic

  • 2 ounces gin
  • 4 to 5​ ounces tonic water
  • Garnish:​ lime wedge

Gather the ingredients. In a highball glass filled with ice cubes, pour the gin then top it with tonic. Stir well. Garnish with lime wedge. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Margarita

  • 1 1/2 ounces tequila
  • 1/2 ounce triple sec
  • 1 ounce lime juice (fresh)
  • Garnish: lime wedge
  • Optional: salt or sugar for rimming

If desired, salt the rim of a chilled cocktail or margarita glass. Pour the ingredients into a cocktail shaker filled with ice cubes. Shake well. Strain into the prepared glass with or without fresh ice. Garnish with a lime wedge. Serve and ENJOY! :)
Frozen margarita is also popular and for a mason jar margarita shake 2 ounces tequila, 1 ounce triple sec, and 3 ounces fresh sour mix with ice. As with any margarita, you can adjust the flavors to suit your own taste.

Moscow Mule

  • 2 ounces vodka
  • 1 ounce lime juice (fresh)
  • 4 ounces ginger beer (or enough to fill)
  • Garnish: lime wedge

Gather the ingredients.Pour the vodka and lime juice into a highball glass with ice cubes. Top off with the ginger beer. Garnish with a lime wedge. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Sex On The Beach

  • 1 1/2 ounces vodka
  • 3/4 ounce ​peach schnapps
  • 1/2 ounce creme de cassis (or ​​Chambord)
  • 2 ounces ​orange juice (or ​pineapple juice)
  • 2 ounces ​cranberry juice
  • 1 tablespoon grenadine
  • Garnish: maraschino cherry, orange slice

Gather the ingredients. Pour all the ingredients into a cocktail shaker with ice cubes. Shake well. Strain into a highball glass. Garnish with the orange slice and maraschino cherry. Serve and ENJOY! :)

Caipirinha

  • 1/2 lime
  • 1/2 to 2 teaspoons sugar (to taste)
  • 2 ounces​ cachaça
  • Garnish: lime wheel

Gather the ingredients. Cut half of a lime into small wedges. Place the lime and sugar into an old-fashioned glass and muddle well. Add a few ice cubes. Top the drink with cachaça. Stir well. Serve and ENJOY! :)
